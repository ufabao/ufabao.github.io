About Me

I am a mathematician and computer scientist. I received my Ph.D in Mathematics from Northwestern University in 2023, advised by Ben Antieau.

Machine Learning Projects

Recently I have been experimenting with using large language models to translate between human written mathematics and LaTeX'd formal mathematics. On my Github there's a toy transformer model built from scratch that can generate LaTeX code that actually compiles (usually) into realistic looking Algebraic Geometry. Here is a pdf example of some of the math it has generated Fake Algebraic Geometry, and if you're interested in the code for the model here is the Github repository Algebraic Geometry Transformer.

I've also been exploring the way that diffusion models learn the topology of their target distribution. I have experimentally shown that score based models learn the shape of the data distribution in a very strong sense. Some toy examples can be seen here: Diffusion of Spheres.

As a converted mathematician I also spend lots of time thinking about PINN's. Here is a very bare bones example of a PINN that learns the fundamental solution of the Laplace equation Laplace Equation.

To see some more of my projects you can go to my Github.

Math Research

In another life I was a research mathematician, focused on p-adic Hodge theory. My thesis is titled 'Topological Restriction Homology is Locally Even in Characteristic p'. The research portion of my thesis has grown into a larger project, joint with Noah Riggenbach, that appears here TR of quasiregular semiperfect rings is even and has been submitted for publication.
